# Communication

We treat communication as part of the work — not admin around the work.

Clients shouldn't have to guess where an update lives, whether something is blocked, or how urgent an issue really is. Clear communication keeps projects moving, protects trust, and surfaces problems before they turn into surprises.

The standard: be direct, be timely, leave a written record someone else can follow later.

---

## Right channel for the right thing

**Email** — formal updates, decisions that need a durable record, anything that may need to be forwarded internally.

**Slack / messaging** — day-to-day coordination, quick questions, lightweight back-and-forth that unblocks work.

**Shared docs** — plans, briefs, feedback, approvals, anything that benefits from comments in context.

**Meetings** — decisions that genuinely need live discussion, sensitive conversations, or issues faster to resolve together than in writing.

---

## Response times

During the workweek: Slack within 4 business hours, email within 1 business day.

If something needs more investigation, acknowledge it quickly, confirm ownership, and say when a fuller answer is coming. Don't go silent while you figure it out.

If something is truly time-sensitive, say so directly. Use the urgent channel. Don't bury it in a long thread and hope someone notices.

---

## Client updates

A useful update covers: what happened since the last check-in, what risks or blockers are open, what decisions need input, and what comes next.

We don't hide bad news behind soft language. We don't wait for a perfect fix before raising a concern. If performance is down, a deadline is at risk, or an assumption has changed — we say so early, and we bring a recommendation with it.

---

## Giving and receiving feedback

Feedback works best when it's specific, timely, and tied to the actual work.

Leave feedback in the doc, thread, or asset where the team can see exactly what needs to change. Vague feedback creates interpretation work before revision work can even start. Useful feedback says what's off, why it matters, and what outcome you want instead.

We'll do the same.

---

## Raising concerns

Surface concerns early, directly, and at the lowest level that can solve them.

If something feels unclear, off-track, or uncomfortable — raise it with the primary point of contact first. Name the concern plainly, include examples if possible. If it's not getting resolved, escalate to agency leadership.

The goal isn't drama. The goal is to fix the problem, agree on a path forward, and rebuild trust fast.
